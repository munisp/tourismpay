How Payments Stay Local
The exact mechanics of how TourismPay keeps 97% of tourist spending in African economies

Traditional Flow — Only $47 Stays

Tourist pays$100.00
International card processor (Visa/MC)−$3.50
Foreign booking platform (Expedia/Booking)−$18.00
Currency conversion (2 hops)−$5.50
International bank transfer (SWIFT)−$7.00
Correspondent bank fees−$4.00
Local payment gateway−$2.50
Settlement delays (float cost)−$2.50
Merchant receives$47.00

TourismPay Flow — $97 Stays

Tourist pays (wallet, mobile money, QR)$100.00
TourismPay platform fee−$2.00
Local settlement (same currency)−$0.50
Currency conversion (if needed)−$0.50
Merchant receives$97.00

Why It Works

Payments stay on local rails. No SWIFT. No correspondent banks. No foreign platform commissions. Currency conversion happens once, at interbank rates, within the TourismPay network.
